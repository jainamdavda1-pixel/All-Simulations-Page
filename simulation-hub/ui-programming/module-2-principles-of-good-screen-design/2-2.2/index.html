📘 Wayfinding & Navigation Theory

Navigation Menus, links, and buttons that allow users to move inside an application.
Signposts Labels, headings, and icons that help users determine where they can go.
Breadcrumbs A textual path (e.g. Home > Academic > Results) showing hierarchy and locations.
Wayfinding Visual indicators (like highlighted links) explaining: "Where am I right now?"
Clear Labels Descriptive button titles (like "Fees") instead of vague titles (like "Money").

📝 Usability Conclusion

Good navigation and wayfinding patterns improve usability by helping users understand their current location, available choices, and correct next steps. Descriptive labels and breadcrumbs prevent users from taking confusing detours, saving effort and reducing frustration.
